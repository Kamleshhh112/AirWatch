Understanding AQI
What Does Your
AQI Number Mean?
0–50
Good
Air quality is perfect. Ideal for all outdoor activities — jogging, cycling, sports.
🟢 Perfect
51–100
Moderate
Acceptable. Unusually sensitive people may experience minor symptoms.
🟡 Ok Ok
101–150
Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups
Children, elderly, and those with respiratory conditions should reduce outdoor activity.
🟠 Caution
151–200
Unhealthy — Danger!
Everyone may be affected. Avoid outdoor activity. Wear N95 masks. Keep windows closed.
🔴 Danger
201–300
Very Unhealthy — Serious Risk
Health emergency for all. No outdoor activity. Use air purifiers indoors.
🟣 Severe
300+
Hazardous — Emergency
Seal all windows. Seek medical help if symptoms appear. Do NOT go outside.
☠️ Hazardous
Health Recommendations
Daily Safety Guide
Based on Your AQI
🫁
Danger Zone Advisory
Wear N95/FFP2 mask outdoors. Avoid exercise and heavy breathing. Keep children and elderly inside. Run air purifiers at max. Seal windows.
👃
Moderate Zone Tips
Reduce outdoor activity time. Asthma/heart patients should take extra care. Consider wearing a light mask if outside for more than 1 hour.
🌿
Safe Zone Activities
Perfect for morning walks, jogging, cycling, and family time! Open windows for fresh air. Enjoy outdoor activities freely.
